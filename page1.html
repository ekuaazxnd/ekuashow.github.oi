Soft, gentle, and cute—these qualities unfold as signs, appearing before us.

Yet they arrive with unease, a quiet descent repeated through unnatural, powerless, and pale acts of copy and paste.

The shojo (girl) is not concrete.

a replicated digital figure, she extends and multiplies endlessly across online culture and space—filling and overflowing.

Her human-like form does not bring her closer to being human,

nor does it offer space to reflect on the position or potential of human agency.

Instead, she stands further away—on the side of signs, mutation, and distortion.

From these signs emerges a form of violence—anxious, powerless—

spreading with each replication of the girl,

softening boundaries,

and unsettling the real.

This challenge is not distant.

It belongs to all of us.
